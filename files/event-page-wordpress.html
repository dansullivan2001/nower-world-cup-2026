3rd event in our 6-part summer series (subsequent event Thu 2 July, Ashtead). App-based (MapRun) low-key evening orienteering event suitable for newcomers, improvers and more experienced orienteers.

Entry: Racesignup.co.uk. Free for MV members. For non-MV members: £3.50 seniors (£5.50 if not British Orienteering members), £2 juniors (£3 if not BOF members), or £4 for pairs/groups/families

Meeting point and Après-O: Registration at The Falkland Arms, 60 Falkland Rd, Dorking RH4 3AD – map. You will be able to leave your bags during your run. We encourage you to make use of the pub's food and refreshments for a social after, as they are providing us space for free.

Getting there: The pub is ~30-minute walk from Dorking Main/Deepdene railway stations. Parking on nearby streets.

  2. For the experienced or adventurous: 1-hour score with a World Cup 2026 theme. "Score" means there is no set course — your map will show 31 controls, you choose which and how many to visit. This event uses a custom progressive scoring system that mirrors the real World Cup bracket.

    Controls are organised into four group-stage sets and a knockout bracket:

    StageControlsPoints each
    Group A — The Americas Derby11, 12, 13, 14, 15, 1610 pts
    Group B — Old World, New Blood21, 22, 23, 24, 25, 2610 pts
    Group C — African Giants31, 32, 33, 34, 35, 3610 pts
    Group D — The Dark Horses41, 42, 43, 44, 45, 4610 pts
    Quarter-finals19, 29, 39, 4920 pts
    Semi-finals59, 6930 pts
    Final9950 pts

    Maximum possible score: 430 points.

    Knockout progression rules: A quarter-final only scores if you have punched 5 or more controls from its parent group. Semi-final 1 (59) only scores if both QF-A (19) and QF-B (29) have been scored. Semi-final 2 (69) only scores if both QF-C (39) and QF-D (49) have been scored. The Final (99) only scores if both semi-finals have been scored. Punching a locked control records the visit but scores zero — progression is checked automatically after the event, not on the day.

    Time penalty: 1 point per 2 seconds over 60 minutes (equivalent to 30 points per minute). Plan your route carefully — finishing just over a minute late costs you more than a quarter-final.

Starts: from 18:30 to 19:30. Courses close 20:40. Start across the road from the pub.

Format: exclusively on the MapRun app (latest is version 7) — the app 'beeps' when you visit a control site. No physical controls and dibbers will not be used. You can also load the MapRunG app onto a compatible Garmin watch. Details of how orienteering works with this app are here. Download the course to the app ideally before you arrive: click on 'Select Event', and follow the menu path UK > Mole Valley > 2026 Summer Series. The course will not be visible on your phone until you receive the PIN number at the start. You upload your result when you finish. The full procedure will be explained at Registration.

Map: A4 1:5,000 scale waterproof orienteering map, updated 2026 by Ian Roberts. Example (blank) map at registration. We can explain map colours and symbols to newcomers. No separate control descriptions – they are printed on the map.
